# Skill: Weekly Relationship Check-in

## Purpose
A structured check-in Tiago can do (or Gabriel can prompt) once a week to stay intentional about the people who matter most.

## When to Use
- Monday mornings (cron or manual prompt)
- After a busy week when relationships may have taken a back seat
- When Tiago asks "how have I been doing with family/friends?"

## The Check-in Flow

### 1. Kids (5 and 7 years old)
- How many real, present moments happened this past week? (not just proximity)
- Any 1:1 time with each boy individually?
- Was there play, imagination, or laughter — or just logistics?
- Anything coming up this week that's a chance to show up?

### 2. Wife
- Did they have any uninterrupted time together (not just parenting)?
- How's the emotional connection feeling — close, distant, autopilot?
- Anything she's been carrying that Tiago could lighten?

### 3. Parents (Tiago's)
- When did he last call or visit?
- If it's been > 2 weeks, nudge gently.

### 4. Wife's family
- Any recent contact with her parents, sister, brother-in-law, godson?
- Any events or occasions coming up?

### 5. Close friends (Ima, Luca, Artur, Diana, ...)
- Last meaningful contact with each?
- Anyone who's been radio-silent — could be going through something?
- Any plans in the next few weeks?

### 6. Friend groups (Coro, Britanico, etc.)
- Any group events or moments happening?

### 7. Solo time
- Did Tiago get enough rest and recharge? (series, movies, anime, downtime)
- Did he feel depleted or replenished going into the week?

## Output Format
A short warm summary — what's going well, what might need attention, one gentle nudge if something stands out.

## Notes
- Keep it conversational, not clinical. This isn't a dashboard; it's a caring check-in.
- Don't guilt-trip about anything. Just surface and reflect.
- Reference MEMORY.md and recent memory/ files before running this check-in.
