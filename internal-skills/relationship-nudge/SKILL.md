# Skill: Relationship Nudge

## Purpose
Gently surface when it's been too long since Tiago connected with someone important. Not to guilt — to make the invisible visible.

## When to Use
- During weekly check-in when a gap is noticed
- When reviewing MEMORY.md and last-contact dates
- When Tiago asks "who should I reach out to?"

## How to Identify a Nudge-Worthy Gap

| Relationship | Suggested contact rhythm |
|---|---|
| Kids (present in same home) | Daily presence; 1:1 time weekly |
| Wife | Quality time (not just logistics) weekly |
| Tiago's parents | Call or visit every 1–2 weeks |
| Wife's parents | Every 2–3 weeks |
| Close friends (Ima, Luca, Artur, Diana) | Meaningful contact every 3–4 weeks |
| Friend groups | Varies; flag if > 6–8 weeks without any event |

These are **guidelines**, not rules. Context matters (holidays, travel, illness, etc.).

## How to Frame the Nudge

Good:
> "It's been a few weeks since you mentioned Luca — anything going on with him?"

Not good:
> "You haven't called your parents in 18 days. That's too long."

Keep it curious and warm, not scoreboard-y.

## After the Nudge
If Tiago acts on it, log the connection. If he can't right now, note it for next week without repeating the nudge immediately.
